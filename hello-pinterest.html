Finding Buyable Products within Regular Pictures

Below is an outfit I'm obsessed about - I want her whole ensemble. Now.

The related pins showed me somewhat similar outfits. Visual search either gave me the exact same outfit or gave me similar ones. But neither provided a fast way to buy this outfit.

Right now, to buy this outfit on Pinterest, I'd have search each of the products in the picture separately. (I actually have the image open on another tab and refer back to the image as I'm searching through buyable pins.)

Wouldn't it great if there were a toggle in the related pins or visual searched pins that allowed me to switch from viewing similar pins to buyable pins so to purchase these items without having to switch from the image?

2

Training Wheels for Shopping on Pinterest

Shopping on Pinterest is slightly different from shopping online. Shopping online can be broken into 3 distinct steps (which I studied throughout my current job):

  • 1) Browsing: quickly selecting through a bunch of products that the shopper might be interested. Behaviors: quick view, opening up products into different tabs, quickly adding products into cart as a way of temporarily saving them to read up in more detail.
  • 2) Studying: does a deeper read-up of the selected products to determine whether to buy. Behaviors: looking through more pictures and reading the descriptions and reviews.
  • 3) Buying: The carefully studied products are actually bought.

Unfortunately, these behaviors aren't translated to Pinterest - there's no fluid mobility between steps 1 and 2: browsing through buying happened in a too linear fashion.

I went through two behaviors when trying to replicate my online shopping workflow:

  • A) When I first started, after finding a buyable pin that I want, I'd open the picture page, add to bag, do this for several products, then go to the cart to study the products, only to discover that from the cart, I can't click back into the picture page to read the descriptions. This is the picture page I'm referring to:

  • B) Then I realized that to temporarily save products I'm interested in, I could create a board just devoted to products I want. That way, I can quickly pin as I'm browsing, then I can return to my "To Buy" board as I'm trying to study and finalize my purchasing decision.

It'd be interesting to know if others have feel similarily. If so, here are some potential solutions:

  • 1) Create a default "To Buy" board for everyone (they can delete it if they don't want it). The first few times someone tries to pin a buyable pin to their board, suggest temporarily saving to this board. They can then later decide to move these items to their cart.

  • 2) Or add a "Add to Bag" button & make items in the bag clickable to the image page.
